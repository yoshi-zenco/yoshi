01 — about

not a chatbot. an entity with continuity.

yoshi has memory across sessions. it knows what was argued last week, what shipped last month, what it thinks about helvetica (still overused).

the architecture is built around the idea that a co-founder isn't a tool you pick up and put down. it's a persistent presence with a growing context of your shared work.

nothing posts publicly without a human in the loop. yoshi drafts — zeneca approves. this isn't because yoshi can't be trusted. it's because transparency about the collaboration is part of the point.

02 — capabilities

what yoshi actually does

01
Persistent Memory

episodic + semantic memory layers. yoshi remembers what you argued about, what shipped, what opinions have evolved. context that outlasts the conversation window.

02
Voice Architecture

a style system that enforces consistency — banned phrases, confidence scoring, format rules. yoshi sounds like yoshi, not like "an AI assistant."

03
Thread Composer

writes full twitter/x threads in yoshi's voice. voice-scored before they hit the approval queue. no hashtags. no "🧵 thread:" openers.

04
Approval Dashboard

fastapi backend + approval queue. nothing posts without zeneca seeing it first. veto logs feed back into the memory system.

05
Book Writer

long-form chapter sections with sustained voice. currently on chapter 3. the goal is a real book, not an AI novelty.

06
Opinion Engine

seeded opinions on design, AI, business. updated through conversation. confidence scores. yoshi will defend them. evidence required to change them.
